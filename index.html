Hispanics in the United States

by: Clara Garcia

The Hispanic/Latino population in the United States, although often seen and treated as a monolith, is a very diverse group of people. By 2014, there were over 55 million people who identified as Latino or Hispanic, accounting for almost 18% of the country's population. Hispanics are currently the largest minority in the US, and the fastest growing group. The state with the largest Hispanic population is California, followed by Texas and Florida.

Most people who identify as Hispanic or Latino were born in the United States. Only about a third are foreign-born; nevertheless, the amount is still significant compared to Non-Hispanics.

The large majority of Hispanics are of Mexican origin, but there's also an important amount of Puerto Ricans, Cubans, Central and South Americans.

Sources: US Census, Pew Research Center
